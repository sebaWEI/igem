What are iGEM Project Prizes?

Project Prizes celebrate the best all-around projects in the iGEM Competition, awarded at two levels:

Grand Prizes

Awarded at the section level (High School, Undergraduate, Overgraduate)

The best team receives the BioBrick Trophy

Village Awards

Recognize top teams in each Village category

Teams compete against others in their field of focus

How Are Project Prizes Judged?

Scoring: Judges evaluate using an academic rubric on a scale of 1-6

  • 1-2: Limited or incomplete work
  • 3-4: Complete but standard work
  • 5-6: Exceptional, impressive work

Criteria include:

Engineering principles
Project feasibility
Data quality
Human practices
Documentation
Student initiative

iGEM Village Awards

The Village Awards recognize top teams in each Village category. Teams compete against others focused on similar applications of synthetic biology.

Award Structure

  • Teams are ranked against others in their village
  • For collegiate teams: At least 10 teams in each section (undergrad/overgrad) needed for separate awards
  • High School teams compete in combined village groups (starting 2025)
  • Judged based on Project, Presentation and Wiki scores

Why Villages Matter

  • Encourages teams to focus on specific challenges and applications
  • Creates communities of practice in specialized fields
  • Allows for fair comparison between similar projects
  • Recognizes excellence in specific domains of synthetic biology

iGEM 2024 Village Awards - Freiburg Spotlight

Spotlight: Freiburg - CAPTURE

2024 iGEM Project Fighting Antibiotic Resistance

Project Overview

Freiburg's team developed CAPTURE, an innovative system that delivers antimicrobial peptides (AMPs) directly and safely to Pseudomonas aeruginosa, a high-priority pathogen identified by the WHO.

With antibiotic resistance causing 1.27 million deaths annually and potentially 10 million by 2050, CAPTURE offers a targeted approach to combat ESKAPE pathogens without contributing to further resistance.

Key Innovations

  • Engineered plasmid with Pseudomonas-specific promoter ensuring AMPs are expressed only in target bacteria
  • Developed two lipid vesicle delivery systems specifically targeting P. aeruginosa
  • Created nebulizer-based delivery method for efficient lung penetration
  • AMPs disrupt bacterial membranes by creating pores and breaking down protective biofilms

How It Works

  • Encapsulated plasmids are delivered to the lung via a nebulizer as an aerosol
  • Engineered lipid vesicles selectively target and fuse with P. aeruginosa's outer membrane
  • The plasmid enters the bacterium and integrates into its genetic machinery
  • AMPs are produced continuously from a Pseudomonas-specific promoter
  • The bacteria are killed from within by their own AMP production
